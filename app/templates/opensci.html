Why This Matters

Open science isn't just a mandate — it's a competitive advantage. Studies consistently show that open access papers receive more citations, shared datasets generate new collaborations, and publicly available code accelerates reproducibility.

This score tracks how well IGB researchers are adopting open practices — not to rank, but to show where we're doing well and where we can improve together.

Five dimensions, weighted by impact:

  • OA Accessibility (30%) — is your work openly readable?
  • Data Openness (25%) — can others find and reuse your data?
  • Code Openness (20%) — is your analysis code available?
  • Repository Licensing (15%) — are your FRED deposits downloadable?
  • Preprint Sharing (10%) — do you share results early?

Scores use empirical Bayes shrinkage so researchers with few publications aren't penalised — they're treated as uncertain. Full methodology →

How to Improve Your Score

High impact
Upload data to FRED with an open license
Use CC-BY or CC0. Only - of FRED deposits are currently downloadable — be the change.
High impact
Publish datasets in data journals
Scientific Data, Earth System Science Data, or Biodiversity Data Journal. Counts toward both your publication record and Data Openness dimension.
Medium impact
Share code on GitHub/GitLab
Link your repository in the paper abstract or methods. Verified DOIs (Zenodo) get full credit; abstract mentions get partial credit.
Medium impact
Choose gold or diamond OA journals
IGB has OA funding support. Green OA (self-archiving) also helps but receives less credit.
Quick win
Post a preprint before peer review
Use bioRxiv, EarthArXiv, or ESSOAr. Takes 10 minutes and makes your work citeable months earlier.
Quick win
Add a data availability statement
Linking to a repository gets partial credit (0.3). "Available upon request" is scored at 0.1 — barely above zero.
